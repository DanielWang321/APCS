Section Summary:
 Post Civil-war, the railroad helps to grow the economy and quickly becomes the largest business nationwide. Land grants allowed railroad companies to inexpensively create a transcontinental railroad, however motivation is largely for money, and the system blocks the most valuable land to settlers. The motivation continues to be cultivated into a monopoly, as businesses now completely rely on the railroad for transport/manufacturing of goods. Operators engage in unfair business practices and aggressive competition, leading to the poor being overcharged with rates because it is unpopulated, and the larger customer getting cheaper costs. Attempts at regulation by the state of Illinois are outruled, however the federal government does pass the Interstate Commerce Act & ICC, and although inefficient it is the first attempt at the federal government regulating private enterprise.
