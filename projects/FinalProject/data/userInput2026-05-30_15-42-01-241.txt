Honors English 3 Final Exam Study Guide
1. MLA Formatting
Basic MLA Setup
✅ 1-inch margins
✅ 12-point Times New Roman
✅ Double-space everything
✅ No extra spacing between heading/title/paragraphs
✅ Left-align text
✅ Indent first line of each paragraph
MLA Heading
Upper left corner:
Your Name
Teacher’s Name
Honors English 3
29 May 2026
Date format: day month year
MLA Header
Upper right corner:
Last Name Page Number
Example: Wang 1
Title Formatting
Center the title. Do not bold, underline, italicize, enlarge, or use quotation marks.

2. Formatting Titles
Type of Work
Format

Poems, short stories, articles, chapters

3. Incorporating Quotations
Main Rule
Quotes must fit into your own sentence.
❌ “The last scud of day holds back for me.”
✅ Whitman writes, “The last scud of day holds back for me.”
Useful Lead-Ins
The narrator states,
Fitzgerald reveals,
The speaker explains,
The author writes,
Quote Punctuation
Periods go after citations:
✅ “I can’t stand it” (Salinger 193).
Question/exclamation marks stay inside the quote:
✅ “What Gatsby?” (Salinger 11).

4. MLA In-Text Citations
Novels
Without author in sentence:
✅ (Salinger 193)
With author in sentence:
✅ (193)
❌ Never use commas: (Cisneros, 24)
Poetry
Use slashes for line breaks:
✅ “search another” (12-13)
Films
✅ (Dead Poets Society)
Online Sources
Use author’s last name or shortened article title:
✅ (Smith)
✅ (“Effects of Censorship”)

5. Block Quotations
Use for quotes 4+ typed lines.
Rules:
✅ Introduce with a colon
✅ Indent 0.5 inch
✅ No quotation marks
✅ Citation comes after punctuation
Poetry block quotes keep original line breaks.

11. Active vs. Passive Voice
Be Verbs
am, are, is, was, were, be, being, been, become, became
Active Voice
Subject performs action.
✅ The city raised the fare.
Passive Voice
Subject receives action.
❌ The fare was raised by the city.
Use stronger verbs whenever possible.

12. Punctuation Rules
Commas
Compound Sentences
Use comma + FANBOYS between two complete sentences.
✅ The restaurant serves pasta, and the desserts are excellent.
Series Comma
✅ He ran, jumped, and slid.
Introductory Elements
✅ After the game, we celebrated.
Parenthetical Elements
✅ The bridge, which spans the river, is old.
Coordinate Adjectives
✅ A tall, distinguished man
Quotations
✅ Peter writes, “The purpose is clear.”
Comma Splice
❌ Two complete sentences joined only by a comma.
Fix with:
FANBOYS
Semicolon
Period
Subordination

Colons
Use after a complete sentence to introduce:
Lists
Quotes
✅ Jenna owns four cats: Angel, Kit, Cinnamon, and Whiskers.

Semicolons
Use between related complete sentences.
✅ The evidence is weak; however, the claim is convincing.
Use in complicated lists with commas.

13. Ethos, Pathos, Logos
Ethos = credibility
Uses experts, fairness, experience.
Pathos = emotion
Uses stories, emotional language, humor.
Logos = logic
Uses facts, statistics, reasoning.
Strong arguments use all three.

14. Descriptive & College App Essays
Strong essays:
✅ Show, not tell
✅ Use sensory details
✅ Focus on meaningful moments
✅ Reveal growth/personality
✅ Avoid vague language
