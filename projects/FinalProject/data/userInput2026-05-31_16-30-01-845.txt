Preterito Rules - Honors Espanol IV Pd. 9
Reglas Del Pretérito (Pasado)

The preterite tells us specifically when an action took place (actions that definitely ended). 
The imperfect tells us in general when an action is taking place (ongoing in the past, no end time).

Ella no pudo hacer su tarea. v.s. Ella no podía hacer su tarea
She couldn't and didn't do her homework v.s. She couldn't do her homework 
Preterite - (end in -ed when translated into English)
The preterite is used in the following situations:
For actions that can be viewed as single events
Ex: Anoche, mi amiga y yo fuimos al cine
Implies completion
For actions that were repeated a specific number of times
Ex. Ayer, practique los verbos irregulares ochenta y ocho veces
For actions that occurred during a specific period of time
Ex. Sahasra estuvo en la casa por dos horas
For actions that were part of a chain of events
Ex. sahasra fue a la recepción, saludó a los novios y salió.
To state the beginning or the end of an action
Ex. La boda empezó a las seis


The preterite is frequently associated with phrases that pinpoint a particular occasion or specific time frame

Ex: Durante dos siglos - for 2 centuries
Ex. Desde ayer - from yesterday
Desde = from (since)
Por = durante, are exact synonyms 
Ex. Hace dos semanas - two weeks ago


Vosotros in the preterite is always the tú form with -is at the end
